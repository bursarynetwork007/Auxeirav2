The compound effect — why each missed window costs more than one
In actuarial terms, policy influence events are not independent. Each successful influence event increases the probability of the next — through credibility, relationship access, and citation momentum. Each missed event reduces it. At Zenex's current ERC-B, the compound probability of being cited in three consecutive DBE budget consultations is 3%. At ERC-A it is 31%. The gap is not linear — it is exponential.

The lapse rate problem — re-engagement is not free
In insurance, lapsed policyholders are significantly more expensive to re-acquire than to retain. The same applies to stakeholder relationships. A Treasury official who has not engaged Zenex's evidence in 24 months requires three to five years of consistent, high-quality, fiscal-language evidence to re-engage at the same level. The cost of attrition is not just the lost windows — it is the re-acquisition cost that follows.
